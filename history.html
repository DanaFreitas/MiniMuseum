The History of Our Museum
John Mason
The original welcome sign.
The story begins in 1920, when Mrs. Mason's grandparents, John and Judy Mason moved to the United States to experiance its wonderful nature. They moved to Lincoln, Idaho, for the natural beauty they count not experiance in their native England.

However, one thing they missed was the centuries old pieces of culture they saw in both museums. So they decided to make their own. Over their lifetimes, they collected a wide variety of items from all over the United States. In 1971, their son Harry Mason obtained ownership of the house after the death of his mother. He had grown up with the collection, but he wanted the world to see it, so in 1973, he made an extension to the and established a museum open to the public, under the name "Mr. Mason's Magnificent Mini-Museum".

Harry Mason
Mabel Mason
The unnamed museum continued until 2002, when Harry died, and his daughter Mabel took over. It was there that she gave the museum a formal name , and so Mrs. Mason's Magnificent Mini-Museum was born. Since then, she has expanded the museum even more and made it more popular than ever. Now where we once had people from all over the country visit, there are people from all over the world.
